Overview

A modest but relevant reconstruction side project

medrecon is a lightweight Streamlit demo for accelerated MRI reconstruction from undersampled k-space. It is intentionally much smaller in scope than the TEM reconstruction system, but it is useful as a clear demonstration of transferable inverse-problem thinking across imaging modalities.

The project focuses on readable implementations, side-by-side comparison of reconstruction methods, and interactive explanation of the measurement model. It is best understood as an exploratory educational tool rather than a research centerpiece or production system.

Methods

What the demo implements

Forward model

Simulates undersampled MRI acquisition in k-space and reconstructs an image from incomplete Fourier measurements.

Classical baseline

Includes zero-filled reconstruction and a pure-NumPy FISTA implementation for sparse regularized recovery.

Learned variants

Adds a simple learned-ISTA baseline and a lightweight U-Net to compare algorithmic and learned approaches in one interface.

Why it belongs in the portfolio: it shows the same general algorithmic instincts as the TEM work, but in a smaller, clearer, easier-to-demo imaging setting.
